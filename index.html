Class 12 History MCQ Quiz

Welcome to Class 12 History MCQs! This page contains important multiple-choice questions from Class 12 History chapters. These MCQs are designed to help students practice for:
✔ CBSE Class 12 Boards
✔ Competitive exams
✔ Entrance tests

Each question includes the correct answer and an explanation. This improves your understanding and makes your concepts stronger.

Answer the questions and click "Show Answer" to check.

1. Who was the founder of the Maurya Empire?

A) Chandragupta Maurya
B) Ashoka
C) Bindusara
D) Chanakya
Correct: A) Chandragupta Maurya
Explanation: Chandragupta Maurya established the empire around 321 BCE with help from Chanakya.

2. The Battle of Plassey was fought in which year?

A) 1757
B) 1764
C) 1857
D) 1947
Correct: A) 1757
Explanation: It marked the beginning of British colonial rule in India.

3. The Great Bath has been found at?

A) lothal
B) Harappa
C) Mohenjo-Daro
D) Dholavira
Correct: C) Mohenjo-Daro
Explanation: The Great Bath is one of the most famous structures of the Indus Valley Civilization, located in Mohenjo-Daro.

4. Which metal was NOT used by Harappans?

A) Gold
B) Silver
C) Iron
D) Copper
Correct: C) Iron
Explanation: The Harappans did not use iron; they primarily used copper, bronze, gold, and silver.

5. Who was the last Mughal emperor?

A) Akbar
B) Aurangzeb
C) Bahadur Shah II
D) Jahangir
Correct: C) Bahadur Shah II
Explanation: He was the last Mughal emperor, deposed by the British in 1857.

6. Harappan script is?

A) Undeciphered
B) Alphabetic
C) Syllabic
D) Ideographic
Correct: A) Undeciphered
Explanation: The Harappan script remains undeciphered despite numerous attempts by scholars.

7.Dholavira is located in?

A) Punjab
B) Gujarat
C) Rajasthan
D) Maharashtra
Correct: B) Gujarat
Explanation: Dholavira is an archaeological site of the Indus Valley Civilization located in the Kutch district of Gujarat.

8.The earliest Tamil texts are known as?

A) Vedas
B) Sangam Literature
C) Upanishads
D) Puranas
Correct: B) Sangam Literature
Explanation: The Sangam literature is the earliest known Tamil literature, dating back to around 300 BCE to 300 CE.

9.The ruling dynasty of Magadha during Mauryan period?

A) Nanda Dynasty
B) Gupta Dynasty
C) Maurya Dynasty
D) Shunga Dynasty
Correct: A) Nanda Dynasty
Explanation: The Nanda Dynasty ruled Magadha before the rise of the Maurya Empire.

10.Ashoka’s inscriptions were written in?

A) Sanskrit
B) Prakrit
C) Pali
D) Tamil
Correct: B) Prakrit
Explanation: Ashoka's inscriptions were primarily written in Prakrit, using the Brahmi script.

11.Mahabharata was originally composed in>

A) Pali
B) Prakrit
C) Sanskrit
D) Tamil
Correct: C) Sanskrit
Explanation: The Mahabharata was originally composed in Sanskrit and is one of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India.

12.The founder of Buddhism is/

A) Mahavira
B) Gautama Buddha
C) Ashoka
D) Chanakya
Correct: B) Gautama Buddha
Explanation: Gautama Buddha is the founder of Buddhism, which originated in India in the 6th century BCE.

13.‘Kitab-ul-Hind’ was written by?

A) Al-Biruni
B) Ibn Battuta
C) Marco Polo
D) Fa-Hien
Correct: A) Al-Biruni
Explanation: Al-Biruni was a Persian scholar who wrote 'Kitab-ul-Hind', a comprehensive account of Indian culture, science, and religion in the 11th century.

14.Kabir was associated with?

A) Bhakti Movement
B) Sufi Movement
C) Jainism
D) Buddhism
Correct: A) Bhakti Movement
Explanation: Kabir was a prominent poet-saint associated with the Bhakti Movement, which emphasized devotion to God and social equality.

15.Vijayanagara Empire was founded in?

A) 1336 CE
B) 1400 CE
C) 1206 CE
D) 1500 CE
Correct: A) 1336 CE
Explanation: The Vijayanagara Empire was founded in 1336 CE by Harihara I and Bukka Raya I.

16.The revolt of 1857 started from?

A) Delhi
B) Lucknow
C) Meerut
D) Kanpur

Explanation: The revolt of 1857, also known as the First War of Indian Independence, began in Meerut on May 10, 1857.

17.Non-Cooperation Movement was launched in?

A) 1915
B) 1920
C) 1930
D) 1942
Correct: B) 1920
Explanation: The Non-Cooperation Movement was launched by Mahatma Gandhi in 1920 as a non-violent protest against British rule in India.

D) Swadeshi Movement

D) 1948
